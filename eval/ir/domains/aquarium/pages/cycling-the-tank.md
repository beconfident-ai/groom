# Cycling the Tank

Before a single fish goes in, the tank must complete the nitrogen cycle — the establishment of bacteria that convert toxic waste into harmless nitrate. The Greenwater Method uses a fishless cycle that typically takes 4 weeks.

Two bacterial colonies do the work. *Nitrosomonas* bacteria convert ammonia (NH3) into nitrite (NO2), and *Nitrospira* bacteria then convert nitrite into nitrate (NO3). Both colonise the biomedia in your [filter](filtration-and-flow.md) and the surface of the [substrate](substrate-and-hardscape.md).

To start a fishless cycle, dose pure ammonia to 2 ppm and keep it there by re-dosing each time it drops. Within 1 to 2 weeks nitrite appears and spikes; by week 3 to 4 the nitrite falls back to zero as the second colony catches up. The cycle is complete when the tank can process 2 ppm of ammonia to zero ammonia and zero nitrite within 24 hours.

Throughout cycling, keep the temperature at 24 °C and run [CO2 and lights](co2-injection.md) normally so plants establish in parallel; live plants speed the cycle by consuming ammonia directly. Fresh aqua soil also leaches ammonia, feeding the colony for free.

Target readings for a cycled, stocked tank are: ammonia 0 ppm, nitrite 0 ppm, and nitrate held near 20 ppm by the weekly [water change](maintenance-schedule.md). Any detectable ammonia or nitrite after stocking means the [biofilter](filtration-and-flow.md) was overwhelmed — stop feeding and change water until it reads zero. Only then add [fish](fish-stocking.md).
