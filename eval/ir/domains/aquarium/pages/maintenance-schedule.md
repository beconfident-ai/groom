# Maintenance Schedule

Consistency beats intensity. The Greenwater Method runs on a fixed weekly rhythm anchored by a single large water change.

**Weekly (Sunday):** change 50 percent of the water. This is the cornerstone of [Estimative Index fertilization](fertilization.md) — the large change resets nutrient build-up so dosing stays predictable. Match the new water's temperature to 24 °C and treat it with dechlorinator at 1 mL per 40 litres before it enters the tank, per [water chemistry](water-chemistry.md). Wipe the front glass, trim fast-growing stems, and thin floating plants on the same day.

**Three times weekly:** dose macronutrients (Mon/Wed/Fri) and micronutrients (Tue/Thu/Sat) on the split schedule from the [fertilization](fertilization.md) page.

**Daily:** feed fish only what they finish in 2 minutes, and glance at the [drop checker](co2-injection.md) to confirm it is lime-green. Top up evaporation with RO water, not tap, so dissolved solids do not creep upward.

**Monthly:** rinse the mechanical filter sponge in old tank water (never tap), push fresh root tabs beside heavy feeders, and test the full panel — pH, KH, GH, nitrate, and phosphate. Clean the [filter](filtration-and-flow.md) intake to prevent the flow drop that breeds [algae](algae-control.md).

**Quarterly:** replace airline tubing and check the CO2 regulator's working pressure. Deep-clean the lily pipes, which fog with biofilm over three months. A tank kept on this schedule rarely sees the problems described in [algae control](algae-control.md).
