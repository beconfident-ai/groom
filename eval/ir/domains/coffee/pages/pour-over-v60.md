# Pour-Over (V60)

The Hario V60 is our reference cone dripper. Its 60-degree wall angle and spiral ribs make flow rate sensitive to grind and pour technique.

## Standard recipe

Our house V60 recipe uses **22 grams of coffee to 360 grams of water**, a 1:16.4 ratio rounded to 1:16 for daily use. See the full [Dose & Ratio Reference](dose-and-ratio.md) for variations. Brew water sits at **94 degrees Celsius** for medium roasts, per the [Brew Temperature](brew-temperature.md) guide.

Grind to a **medium-fine consistency, 600 microns**, equivalent to setting 18 on the Comandante C40, as listed in the [Grind Size Guide](grind-size.md).

## Pour schedule

We pour in four pulses. The **bloom uses 50 grams of water for 45 seconds**, agitating the bed to release CO2. Then pour to 150 g, 250 g, and finally 360 g, finishing all pours by 1:45. Total brew time targets **2 minutes 45 seconds** from first pour to drawdown completion.

A correctly dialed V60 yields an extraction of **20 to 22 percent**, measured per the [Extraction & TDS Measurement](extraction-tds.md) page. If drawdown finishes before 2:15 the cup will taste sour; grind finer. If it stalls past 3:30, grind coarser. The [Troubleshooting](troubleshooting.md) page covers channeling and clogging in detail.

Use a flat, even bed and the gooseneck kettle described in [Grinders & Equipment](equipment.md).
