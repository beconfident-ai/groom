# Dose & Ratio Reference

The brew ratio is the weight of dry coffee to the weight of water. We always measure both on a scale to 0.1 gram, never by volume. A lower ratio number means a stronger, more concentrated cup.

## Ratios by method

- **Pour-over V60 — 1:16**, our daily ratio (22 g coffee to 360 g water). See [Pour-Over (V60)](pour-over-v60.md).
- **French press — 1:15** (60 g to 900 g). See [French Press](french-press.md).
- **AeroPress — 1:13** (16 g to 208 g), our most concentrated filter recipe. See [AeroPress](aeropress.md).
- **Espresso — 1:2** (18 g to 36 g). See [Espresso](espresso.md).
- **Cold brew concentrate — 1:8** (125 g to 1000 g), then diluted 1:1 before serving. See [Cold Brew](cold-brew.md).

## Strength adjustments

To strengthen a brew, lower the ratio number (more coffee per gram of water); to weaken it, raise the number. For espresso variants, a **ristretto is 1:1.5** (18 g to 27 g) and a **lungo is 1:3** (18 g to 54 g).

Changing the ratio shifts **strength** (how concentrated the cup is), while changing grind and time shifts **extraction** (how much you pull from the grounds). Keep these separate when dialing in. The [Extraction & TDS Measurement](extraction-tds.md) page explains how strength and extraction relate to the final TDS reading. Pair every recipe with the matching grind from the [Grind Size Guide](grind-size.md).
