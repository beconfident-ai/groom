# Lighting for Aquatic Plants

Light intensity is measured as PAR (Photosynthetically Active Radiation) at the substrate. The Greenwater Method classes tanks by PAR at the substrate surface: low-tech tanks run 30 to 50 PAR, while high-tech CO2-injected tanks run 60 to 80 PAR. Never exceed 90 PAR at the substrate without exceptional CO2 and dosing discipline, or carpet plants will be outcompeted by [algae](algae-control.md).

Use a full-spectrum LED fixture with a colour temperature of 6500 K, which renders plant greens accurately and drives strong photosynthesis. A spectrum weighted toward red (around 660 nm) and blue (around 450 nm) maximises chlorophyll absorption; avoid fixtures heavy in green wavelengths, which plants reflect rather than use.

The standard photoperiod is 7 hours per day. A shorter, intense burst beats a long, dim one for limiting algae. Resist the urge to extend beyond 8 hours — longer photoperiods feed algae far more than plants. Ramp the fixture up and down over 30 minutes at each end to mimic dawn and dusk and reduce livestock stress.

Mount the fixture 25 to 30 cm above the water surface for even spread across a standard tank. Coordinate the light timer with your [CO2 injection](co2-injection.md) schedule and [fertilization](fertilization.md) dosing so that carbon and nutrients are available exactly when the lights demand them. Demanding red carpeting species need the full 80 PAR; low-light ferns are happy at 30 PAR.
