# The Ember Pool

Embercrown resolves every uncertain action with the **Ember Pool**, a handful of six-sided dice (d6) called embers. You never roll a single die in isolation; you always build and roll a pool.

## Building the pool

Your pool size equals the relevant **Attribute plus Skill**. For example, a swing of an axe rolls Grit + Melee. A character with Grit 3 and Melee 2 rolls a pool of **five d6**. See [Attributes](attributes.md) for the five attributes and [Character Creation](character-creation.md) for skills.

## Counting results

After rolling, read each die:

- A result of **5 or 6 counts as a spark** — a success.
- A **6 is also a blaze**. Each blaze lets you reroll one additional die immediately, and that reroll can chain.
- Results of 1 through 4 are inert and count for nothing.

## Difficulty

Every task has a difficulty equal to the **number of sparks required**. A trivial task needs 1 spark; a Routine task needs 2 sparks; a Hard task needs 3 sparks; a Heroic task needs 4 sparks. The default difficulty for an unlisted action is **2 sparks**.

If you roll **zero sparks on a pool of three or more dice, that is a Fumble**, and the table introduces a complication. Rolling **four or more sparks on any check is a Surge**, granting an extra benefit chosen by the player.

Spending one point of [Momentum](attributes.md) lets you add a single ember to any pool before rolling.
