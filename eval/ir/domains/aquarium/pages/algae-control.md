# Algae Identification and Control

Algae is a symptom, not a disease — almost always an imbalance between [light](lighting.md), [CO2](co2-injection.md), and [nutrients](fertilization.md). Identify the type before treating.

**Black beard algae (BBA)** appears as dark tufts on leaf edges and hardscape. Its single root cause is fluctuating or insufficient CO2; stabilise injection at 30 ppm and BBA stops spreading. Spot-treat existing growth with liquid carbon dosed at 1 mL per 10 litres directly onto the tufts with the filter off for 10 minutes.

**Green spot algae** forms hard dots on glass and old leaves and signals low phosphate. Raise PO4 toward the 3 ppm target and the spots stop returning; scrape the glass with a blade.

**Diatoms** are a brown dust coating that blankets new tanks during the first month. They are harmless and vanish on their own once the [cycle](cycling-the-tank.md) matures; an *Otocinclus* crew clears them fast.

**Green hair algae** indicates too much light relative to CO2. Shorten the [photoperiod](lighting.md) to 6 hours for two weeks and increase CO2, and manually pull the strands out with a toothbrush.

**Green water** (a pea-soup bloom of free-floating algae) is caused by an ammonia spike, usually from disturbing the [substrate](substrate-and-hardscape.md). A 3-day total blackout — covering the tank so no light enters — clears it reliably. The universal preventives are stable CO2, a 7-hour photoperiod, and the weekly [water change](maintenance-schedule.md).
