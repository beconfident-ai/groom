# Operation: ITERATE

One focused quality cycle. Find the single weakest page and make it genuinely good.

1. Read `wiki/index.md` and skim every page (frontmatter + first paragraph).
2. Score pages mentally on: explanation depth (does it teach the *why*?), citation quality,
   internal linking, freshness, and whether a Head of AI could act on it.
3. Pick the **one** weakest page. In your summary, say why it lost.
4. Improve it properly:
   - Rewrite shallow sections with mechanism-level explanations.
   - Add concrete examples where abstractions float free.
   - Verify its claims against the rest of the wiki (fix contradictions in BOTH places).
   - If its topic has moved on, do a targeted WebSearch to refresh it.
   - Strengthen links: it should link to 2+ pages, and 2+ pages should link to it.
5. Bump `updated`, recheck `confidence` honestly.

Do not touch other pages except to fix contradictions or add inbound links.

Finish with a one-paragraph summary: which page, why, what changed.
