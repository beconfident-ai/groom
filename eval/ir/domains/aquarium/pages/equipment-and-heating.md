# Equipment and Heating

A planted tank is a coordinated system of timed hardware. The heater is the first piece: size it at 1 watt per litre, so a 40 litre tank uses a 40 watt heater. Set the thermostat to 24 °C to match the [water chemistry](water-chemistry.md) target, and verify it with a separate glass thermometer mounted on the opposite end of the tank — built-in heater dials drift by up to 2 °C. Place the heater in the filter return flow so heat distributes evenly.

The [CO2](co2-injection.md) hardware chain runs: pressurised cylinder, a dual-gauge regulator, a solenoid valve, a needle valve, a bubble counter, and finally the diffuser. Set the working pressure to 1.5 bar for a ceramic diffuser. The solenoid must be on the same timer circuit as nothing else, opening 1 hour before lights and closing 1 hour before lights-out.

Three timers run the tank: one for the [light](lighting.md) fixture (7-hour photoperiod), one for the CO2 solenoid, and one for a night-time air stone that runs only during the dark hours to replace oxygen. Stagger nothing else onto these circuits.

A pressurised CO2 cylinder of 2 litres lasts roughly 6 months on a 40 litre tank at 2 bubbles per second. Keep a refill on hand. Inspect all silicone tubing quarterly for hardening, and replace the check valve yearly so water never siphons back into the regulator. Combine this hardware with the [maintenance schedule](maintenance-schedule.md) for a trouble-free tank.
