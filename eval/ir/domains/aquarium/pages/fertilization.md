# Fertilization and Dosing

This wiki uses the Estimative Index (EI) approach: dose nutrients in slight excess so plants are never limited, then reset accumulation with a weekly water change. The whole scheme assumes the 40 PAR-plus [lighting](lighting.md) and 30 ppm [CO2](co2-injection.md) described elsewhere; without those, EI dosing simply feeds [algae](algae-control.md).

Target weekly water-column concentrations for a high-tech tank are: nitrate (NO3) 20 ppm, phosphate (PO4) 3 ppm, and potassium (K) 30 ppm. Dose macronutrients on Monday, Wednesday, and Friday, splitting the weekly total into three equal additions. For a 40 litre tank that means roughly 5 mL of the macro stock solution per dose.

Micronutrients (iron and trace elements) are dosed on Tuesday, Thursday, and Saturday — never on the same day as phosphate, because PO4 and iron will precipitate together and fall out of solution. Target iron (Fe) at 0.5 ppm in the water column. The standard micro dose is 3 mL for a 40 litre tank.

Sunday is the rest-and-reset day: perform the [water change](maintenance-schedule.md) and dose nothing. The large change zeroes out any nutrient build-up so the cycle restarts clean each week.

Stem plants showing pinholes in older leaves signal a potassium deficiency; pale new growth points to iron shortage. Always feed the [aquatic plants](aquatic-plants.md) according to their growth rate — fast growers strip the column quickly.
