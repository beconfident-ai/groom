# CO2 Injection

Carbon dioxide is the single largest growth lever in a high-tech tank. The Greenwater Method targets a dissolved CO2 concentration of 30 ppm during the photoperiod. The most reliable way to confirm this is a drop checker filled with 4 dKH reference solution: at 30 ppm it turns a clear lime-green. A yellow checker signals dangerous overdosing (above 40 ppm); a blue checker means CO2 is too low (below 15 ppm).

Inject through a pressurised regulator into an inline atomiser or a fine ceramic diffuser. For a 40 litre tank, a starting rate of 2 bubbles per second through the bubble counter is typical; scale to 3 bubbles per second for a 60 litre tank. Always begin CO2 **1 hour before** the lights switch on so the target concentration is reached by the time photosynthesis ramps up, and shut the solenoid off **1 hour before** lights-out to conserve gas.

Surface agitation drives off CO2, so reduce flow at the surface during injection hours. Pair injection with strong [lighting](lighting.md) and full-column [fertilization](fertilization.md) — running high light without matching CO2 is the most common cause of [algae](algae-control.md).

Watch livestock closely: fish gasping at the surface indicate the CO2 is too high and oxygen too low. Keep an air stone on a timer for the dark hours. The pH should drop roughly 1.0 unit from its degassed value when 30 ppm is dissolved — see [water chemistry](water-chemistry.md) for buffering targets.
