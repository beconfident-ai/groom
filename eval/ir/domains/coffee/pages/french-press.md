# French Press

The French press is a full-immersion method: coffee steeps in water for the entire brew, then a metal mesh plunger separates the grounds. It produces a heavier body than filter methods because fines and oils pass through the screen.

## Standard recipe

Our reference uses a **1:15 ratio**, for example 60 grams of coffee to 900 grams of water in a 1-liter press. Grind **coarse, 1000 microns**, which is setting 30 on the Comandante C40 (see [Grind Size Guide](grind-size.md)). Coarse grounds limit fines and make the plunger easier to press.

Pour water at **96 degrees Celsius**, the hottest of our recipes, because the immersion temperature drops quickly in the tall vessel. Compare temperatures on the [Brew Temperature](brew-temperature.md) page.

## Steep and plunge

Steep for a **total of 8 minutes**. At the 4-minute mark, break the crust by stirring the floating grounds twice and skim the foam off the surface. Let it rest the remaining 4 minutes so fines settle, then **plunge slowly over 20 seconds**. Decant immediately to stop extraction.

A well-made French press extracts to roughly **19 to 21 percent**. Because there is no paper filter, the cup carries more sediment; let it stand 30 seconds before serving so grit settles. For thin or muddy results, see the [Troubleshooting](troubleshooting.md) page, and review [Dose & Ratio Reference](dose-and-ratio.md) to adjust strength.
