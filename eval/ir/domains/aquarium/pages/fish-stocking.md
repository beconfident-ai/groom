# Fish Stocking and Livestock

The Greenwater Method follows a conservative stocking rule of 1 cm of adult fish length per 2 litres of water in a planted tank — half the density of an unplanted setup, because dense plants and [CO2 injection](co2-injection.md) reduce the oxygen margin. A 40 litre tank therefore holds about 20 cm of fish.

Choose small schooling species. Neon tetras (*Paracheirodon innesi*) are the signature fish of this wiki; keep them in groups of at least 8, in water of 23 to 25 °C, which overlaps perfectly with the planted-tank target of 24 °C from [water chemistry](water-chemistry.md). Ember tetras and chili rasboras work equally well and stay under 2 cm.

For the bottom, a group of 6 corydoras catfish (*Corydoras pygmaeus*) sifts the substrate and eats leftover food. Add one *Otocinclus* per 10 litres as a gentle algae-grazing crew; see [algae control](algae-control.md). Avoid large or digging cichlids, which uproot a carpet overnight.

Shrimp deserve special mention: Amano shrimp (*Caridina multidentata*) are the best algae-eaters and tolerate the planted-tank range, but they are sensitive to copper, so verify any [fertilizer](fertilization.md) trace mix is shrimp-safe before dosing. Stock 1 Amano shrimp per 5 litres for effective cleanup.

Always add livestock only after the tank is fully [cycled](cycling-the-tank.md), and never add more than 25 percent of the final stock in a single week so the biofilter can keep pace.
