# Grinders & Equipment

Good equipment removes variables. Our recipes assume the gear below; substitutions are fine as long as you can hit the same grind microns, temperature, and weight precision.

## Grinders

- **Comandante C40** — our reference hand grinder for all filter methods. Each click moves roughly 30 microns, and its conical steel burrs give a tight particle distribution. Settings for each method are on the [Grind Size Guide](grind-size.md).
- **Niche Zero** — our reference espresso grinder, used at setting 12 for the [Espresso](espresso.md) recipe. Its 63 mm conical burrs and near-zero retention suit single dosing.

Always use a **burr grinder**, never a blade grinder, which produces inconsistent particles.

## Kettles, scales, and filters

- **Gooseneck kettle** with variable temperature control to the degree, required for the controlled pours on the [Pour-Over (V60)](pour-over-v60.md).
- **Brewing scale** accurate to **0.1 gram** with a built-in timer; we weigh both dose and water for every brew, per [Dose & Ratio Reference](dose-and-ratio.md).
- **Paper filters** for V60 and AeroPress; rinse them with hot water before brewing to remove papery taste and preheat the brewer.
- **TDS meter and refractometer** for measuring water and beverage solids, covered on [Water Chemistry](water-chemistry.md) and [Extraction & TDS Measurement](extraction-tds.md).

Descale espresso and kettle equipment monthly if your [Water Chemistry](water-chemistry.md) runs above the 150 ppm TDS target. Worn burrs are a hidden cause of inconsistency; see [Troubleshooting](troubleshooting.md).
