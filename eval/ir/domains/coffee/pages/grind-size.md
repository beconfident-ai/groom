# Grind Size Guide

Grind size is the single biggest lever on extraction. Finer grounds expose more surface area and extract faster; coarser grounds extract slower. We specify grind in microns so settings transfer between grinders.

## Particle size by method

The Comandante C40 is our reference hand grinder; clicks below are from fully closed (zero).

- **Espresso — 250 microns**, Niche Zero setting 12. The finest grind we use; see [Espresso](espresso.md).
- **AeroPress — 700 microns**, Comandante setting 22; see [AeroPress](aeropress.md).
- **Pour-over V60 — 600 microns** (medium-fine), Comandante setting 18; see [Pour-Over (V60)](pour-over-v60.md).
- **French press — 1000 microns** (coarse), Comandante setting 30; see [French Press](french-press.md).
- **Cold brew — 1200 microns** (extra-coarse), Comandante setting 35; see [Cold Brew](cold-brew.md).

## Distribution and burrs

A quality conical or flat **burr grinder** is essential; blade grinders produce uneven particles that cause both over- and under-extraction in the same cup. We accept a maximum of **15 percent fines** (particles under 100 microns) for filter grinds.

Each step on the Comandante C40 moves roughly **30 microns**, so a one-click change is a meaningful adjustment for espresso but minor for cold brew. Burr details and recommended grinders live on [Grinders & Equipment](equipment.md). When a cup tastes wrong, adjust grind before changing anything else — the [Troubleshooting](troubleshooting.md) page explains why.
