# Combat

Combat in Embercrown unfolds in rounds called **exchanges**, each representing **6 seconds** of action.

## The Draw

Initiative is called **the Draw**. At the start of combat every participant rolls **1d6 + Finesse**, and play proceeds from highest to lowest. Ties are broken by higher Finesse, then by a single contested d6 roll.

## Actions per exchange

On your turn you may take **one Action, one Move, and one Reaction** each exchange. A Move covers up to **6 metres**. Common Actions include attacking, casting a spell from the [Magic System](magic.md), or using a Calling feature. A Reaction happens outside your turn, such as a parry or an attack of opportunity.

## Attacking

An attack rolls the appropriate [Ember Pool](dice-system.md) — Grit + Melee for melee, Finesse + Marksmanship for ranged. The difficulty to hit equals the target's **Defense**, which is **7 + Finesse** as noted in [Attributes](attributes.md). Because Defense is a target number and not a sparks count, you instead need a number of sparks equal to **2 plus 1 for every full 2 points the target's Defense exceeds 9**.

## Damage

A landed hit deals **weapon dice + 1 per spark beyond the first**. A standard sword deals **2d6**; a dagger deals **1d6**; a greataxe deals **3d6**. Damage is subtracted from the target's Vigor.

## Cover and range

Light cover raises a target's effective Defense by **2**; heavy cover by **4**. Ranged attacks beyond a weapon's listed range take a **−2 dice penalty**. Falling to 0 Vigor is covered in [Conditions & Healing](conditions.md).
