# Substrate and Hardscape

A nutrient-rich active substrate is the engine room of a planted tank. The Greenwater Method specifies a commercial aqua soil laid at a depth of 5 cm at the front, sloping up to 8 cm at the back to create visual depth and give root-feeders room. Aqua soil actively lowers pH and softens water, which is why it pairs with the slightly acidic targets in [water chemistry](water-chemistry.md).

Fresh aqua soil leaches ammonia for the first three weeks, which actually helps kick-start the [nitrogen cycle](cycling-the-tank.md) but means livestock must wait. Do not disturb or vacuum the soil during this leaching window. The soil's nutrient charge lasts roughly 18 months before it becomes inert and must be supplemented with [fertilizer](fertilization.md) or replaced.

For carpeting plants, cap a thin 1 cm layer of fine gravel over the soil only if you keep digging fish; otherwise plant carpets directly into the soil for best root contact. Push monthly root tabs 3 cm deep beside heavy root-feeders such as the Amazon sword from [aquatic plants](aquatic-plants.md).

Hardscape uses inert materials only. Seiryu stone raises hardness slightly, so account for it when targeting GH. Spider wood and Manzanita are the preferred woods; both leach tannins that tint water yellow for the first month, harmless but best cleared with the weekly [water change](maintenance-schedule.md). Boil new wood for 30 minutes to sink it and kill surface spores.
