# Extraction & TDS Measurement

Extraction yield is the percentage of the dry coffee's mass that dissolves into the cup. We measure it with a refractometer that reads beverage **TDS** (total dissolved solids), then calculate yield from TDS, brew weight, and dose.

## The formula

Extraction yield is computed as:

**Extraction % = (beverage TDS × brewed beverage weight) ÷ dose**

For example, a V60 brewing 22 g of coffee into 320 g of beverage at 1.38 percent TDS extracts about 20 percent. Beverage TDS is a different measurement from water TDS, which targets 150 ppm on the [Water Chemistry](water-chemistry.md) page.

## The ideal window

Our target extraction window is **18 to 22 percent** for filter coffee. Below 18 percent the coffee is **under-extracted** and tastes sour, salty, and sharp. Above 22 percent it is **over-extracted** and tastes bitter, dry, and hollow.

Filter beverage TDS should land between **1.30 and 1.45 percent**; espresso is far more concentrated at **8 to 12 percent TDS** because of its 1:2 ratio. This pairing of strength (TDS) on one axis and extraction (yield) on the other is the classic **brew control chart**.

If a cup falls outside the window, change grind first, then temperature — never ratio, which only moves strength.
