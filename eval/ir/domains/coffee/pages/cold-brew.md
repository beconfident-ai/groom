# Cold Brew

Cold brew steeps coarse grounds in cool water for many hours. Low temperature extracts slowly and skips many of the acids and aromatics that hot water pulls, yielding a smooth, low-acid concentrate.

## Standard recipe

We brew a **concentrate at a 1:8 ratio**: 125 grams of coffee to 1000 grams of water. Grind **extra-coarse, 1200 microns**, the coarsest setting we use, equal to setting 35 on the Comandante C40 (see [Grind Size Guide](grind-size.md)). A coarse grind keeps the long steep from over-extracting and bitter.

There is no hot water here. Use **room-temperature filtered water at 20 degrees Celsius**; the [Brew Temperature](brew-temperature.md) page lists cold brew as the only ambient method. Stir once to fully wet the grounds.

## Steep and dilute

**Steep for 16 hours** at room temperature, or 18 hours if refrigerated, since cold slows extraction. Filter through a paper-lined sieve to remove all fines. The result is a strong concentrate.

To serve, **dilute the concentrate 1:1 with water or milk** over ice. The concentrate keeps refrigerated for up to 10 days. Because extraction is slow and incomplete, cold brew lands around **18 percent extraction** despite the long contact time. If your concentrate tastes flat or weak, increase the dose toward 1:7 per [Dose & Ratio Reference](dose-and-ratio.md), or check the grind on [Troubleshooting](troubleshooting.md).
