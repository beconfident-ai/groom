# AeroPress

The AeroPress combines immersion steeping with gentle plunger pressure through a paper microfilter. It is forgiving and fast, which makes it our travel and tasting standard.

## Standard recipe

We use the **inverted method** with a **1:13 ratio**: 16 grams of coffee to 208 grams of water. Grind **medium, 700 microns**, setting 22 on the Comandante C40 (see [Grind Size Guide](grind-size.md)). Use a single paper filter rinsed with hot water to remove papery taste.

Brew water is **88 degrees Celsius** here — cooler than our other methods because the short, pressurized contact extracts efficiently and hot water would over-extract. The full table is on [Brew Temperature](brew-temperature.md).

## Steep and press

Add coffee, pour all 208 g of water, and **steep for 90 seconds**. Stir 5 times at the 60-second mark to settle the bed. Attach the cap, flip onto the cup, and **press slowly over 30 seconds** until you hear the hiss. Stop before pressing air through, which adds bitterness.

This recipe targets an extraction of **20 to 21 percent** and a concentrated cup you can dilute with 60 grams of hot water to taste, an Americano-style finish. For a milder brew, drop to a 1:15 ratio per [Dose & Ratio Reference](dose-and-ratio.md). Excess bitterness usually means you pressed too hard or too long; see [Troubleshooting](troubleshooting.md).
