# Water Chemistry for Planted Aquariums

A stable, slightly acidic water column is the foundation of the Greenwater Method used across this wiki. Target a pH of 6.6, holding it within the 6.4 to 6.8 band throughout the photoperiod. Carbonate hardness (KH) should sit at 4 dKH, with an acceptable range of 3 to 5 dKH; this buffers against the pH crash that aggressive [CO2 injection](co2-injection.md) can otherwise trigger. General hardness (GH) belongs in the 4 to 6 dGH window, supplying the calcium and magnesium that stem plants need for firm new growth. We aim for a Ca:Mg ratio of 3:1.

Total dissolved solids (TDS) should read between 120 and 180 ppm on a freshly water-changed tank, drifting no higher than 220 ppm before the next change. If your tap water exceeds 250 ppm TDS, cut it 50/50 with remineralised RO water.

Temperature is held at 24 °C, the sweet spot that keeps oxygen solubility high while satisfying both plants and most community fish. Never let the tank exceed 27 °C, as warmer water holds less dissolved oxygen and accelerates the [algae](algae-control.md) we work to avoid.

Test KH and GH weekly with a titration kit; a single drop of reagent equals 1 dKH or 1 dGH. Chlorine and chloramine from tap water must be neutralised with a dechlorinator dosed at 1 mL per 40 litres before any water touches the tank.
