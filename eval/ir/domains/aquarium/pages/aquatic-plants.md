# Aquatic Plants and Their Requirements

Plants are grouped by their position and demand. Foreground carpeting species are the most demanding: *Glossostigma elatinoides* and dwarf hairgrass (*Eleocharis acicularis*) both need the full 80 PAR and 30 ppm [CO2](co2-injection.md) to grow flat and tight rather than reaching upward. Plant carpets in small clumps spaced 2 cm apart for fastest fill-in.

Midground rhizome plants are far more forgiving. Java fern (*Microsorum pteropus*) and *Anubias barteri* attach to hardscape and thrive at just 30 PAR with no added CO2. Critically, their rhizome must never be buried in the substrate — bury it and the plant rots within two weeks. Tie them to wood or rock with thread until they anchor.

Background stem plants such as *Rotala rotundifolia* grow up to 4 cm per week under high light and turn deep red when nitrate is kept low and iron high; see [fertilization](fertilization.md). Trim stems at a node and replant the cut tops to thicken a bush.

The Amazon sword (*Echinodorus bleheri*) is a heavy root-feeder; tuck a root tab into the [substrate](substrate-and-hardscape.md) beside it monthly. Floating plants like *Salvinia* shade the tank and consume excess nitrate, a useful tool against [algae](algae-control.md), but they must be thinned weekly or they block all light to plants below. Match every species to the tank's [water chemistry](water-chemistry.md) before planting.
