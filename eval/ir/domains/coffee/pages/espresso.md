# Espresso

Espresso forces hot water through a compacted puck of finely ground coffee under high pressure, producing a concentrated shot topped with crema. It is the least forgiving method and demands a precise grinder.

## Standard recipe

Our reference shot is a **1:2 ratio**: an **18-gram dose** in a double basket yielding **36 grams** of liquid espresso. Grind **fine, 250 microns** — far finer than any filter method — using the Niche Zero at setting 12, as noted in [Grind Size Guide](grind-size.md).

Pull the shot at **9 bars of pressure** with a group temperature of **93 degrees Celsius** (see [Brew Temperature](brew-temperature.md)). The target **shot time is 28 seconds** from the moment the pump engages until you stop at 36 g out.

## Dialing in

If the shot runs faster than 25 seconds it will taste sour and thin — grind finer. If it chokes past 32 seconds and drips, grind coarser. A dialed shot extracts to **19 to 20 percent** with a syrupy body and persistent crema.

Distribution matters: use a WDT tool to break clumps, then tamp level with about 15 kg of force. Channeling — water finding a fast path through the puck — is the most common espresso fault and is covered on [Troubleshooting](troubleshooting.md). For milk drinks, the same 36 g shot pairs with steamed milk; see [Dose & Ratio Reference](dose-and-ratio.md) for ristretto and lungo variants.
